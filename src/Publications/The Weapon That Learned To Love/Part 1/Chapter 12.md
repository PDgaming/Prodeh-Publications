Conversations remained subdued, as if everyone had agreed not to disturb the music. The light barely reached the corners of the room, but it didn’t need to; everything that mattered was gathered near the stage.

David loved the soulful improvisations coming from the stage. Surprisingly, Robert seemed to enjoy them too, tapping his foot to the lively saxophone solo. David smiled and reached across the small, candlelit table to briefly cover Robert's hand. Robert instinctively tightened his fingers around David's.

When the act concluded, David began to notice a subtle shift in Robert's demeanor. His eyes, usually fixed on David or the stage, started to dart around. First, he quickly scanned the entrance. Then, his gaze lingered in the shadows behind the bar.

Robert excused himself to the restroom, glancing at every face he passed. David noticed his shoulders subtly tensing as he made his way to the back of the club. When he returned, his posture was a little stiffer, and his smile appeared a bit too fixed. David caught him scanning the street outside the window more than once, his gaze piercing the dim reflections.

"Everything alright?" David asked gently, leaning closer over the table during a quiet break in the music. "You seem a bit... distracted tonight."

Robert's gaze snapped back to David, a flicker of something-alarm, perhaps-crossing his eyes before a smooth, almost practiced calm settled over his features. "Just tired," Robert replied, offering a small, dismissive shrug. "Long week at work." He forced a relaxed smile, but David noticed a subtle tremor in his hand as he lifted his drink. David didn't push; he wanted to believe it was just a result of fatigue.

They left soon after, with Robert unusually quiet during the walk home. When they reached David's house, the familiar warmth of the home was a welcome embrace.

David switched on the living room lights, the soft glow chasing away the last remnants of the evening. They settled onto the couch, the cushions still warm from their earlier presence. David leaned his head back, letting out a soft sigh.

"So," David began casually, trying to keep the atmosphere light, even though his curiosity had turned into a prickling sensation beneath his skin. "What's going on, Robert? You've been... different tonight. Is there anything you want to talk about?" He glanced at Robert, whose gaze was fixed on the far wall, his jaw tight.

Robert shifted slightly, a barely perceptible movement. "Nothing," he murmured, his voice low and dismissive. "Just... a long day. You worry too much."

David frowned. The refusal to meet his eyes, the subtle tension in his shoulders; these were not the traits of the Robert he had spent months getting to know. A slow frustration began to simmer beneath David's concern. He was about to press him, to tell him he deserved better than vague excuses and that he was here to listen-

A sharp, insistent knock echoed against the front door, vibrating through the floorboards and making David startle. Robert, beside him, froze. David watched Robert's eyes widen, a fleeting flicker of panic crossing his face-just for a breath-before it vanished, replaced by an unnerving stillness. Robert's body went rigid, all casualness evaporating.

The sound shattered the quiet. David looked at Robert, then back at the door. "Who on earth...?" he muttered, already rising from the couch. He glanced back at Robert, expecting him to join, but Robert remained seated, his gaze fixed on the front door.

David approached the door cautiously, peering through the peephole. A tall man in dark clothing stood on the porch, his face partially hidden beneath the brim of a black cap. He seemed ordinary enough, but something about his stillness felt unsettling.

David's hand hovered over the lock, an illogical chill running down his spine. He turned on the porch light and opened the door a crack. "Can I help you?" he asked, trying to keep his tone firm despite the unexpected tremor in his stomach.

"I'm looking for Robert." The man said, his voice low and steady, each word measured and precise, cutting through the night air. His eyes, though shadowed by the cap, seemed to bore into the space behind David.

David's confusion sharpened into a cold spike of alarm. He glanced back at Robert, who was now slowly rising from the couch, his movements deliberate. The casual, kind man David knew had vanished. Robert's eyes, usually warm and soft, had turned cold, serious, almost black. His lips were pressed into a thin, grim line, and David could see the subtle clenching of his hands at his sides.

"Do you know him, Robert?" David asked, his voice steady as he unconsciously positioned himself between Robert and the stranger.

Robert stepped forward, moving silently, remarkably quiet for someone of his size. He positioned himself directly in front of David, his broad shoulder forming a solid, almost immovable presence. It was both comforting and, for some reason, David couldn't quite explain, unsettling. David had never seen him like this; so still and so certain. He looked up at Robert's face, noting the hard set of his jaw and the intense focus in his eyes.

"I don't know who he is," Robert replied, his voice flat and steady, though the lie tasted bitter on David's tongue, echoing his earlier suspicion.

The man on the porch tilted his head slightly, a mix of disappointment and resigned patience on his face. "We need to talk, Williams." He said finally, his tone calm, almost conversational, but the weight of his words was anything but. His gaze remained locked on Robert, unwavering. "We didn't have to come here, but you didn't respond. We expected better from you."

Robert's fists clenched at his sides. He stood there, unmoving, a living barrier. David's own heart pounded in his chest. He didn't fully understand the exchange, but the danger was palpable.

"You've got the wrong house. I suggest you leave." David replied, his voice steely now, eyes narrowed on the stranger.

The man's gaze softened for a fraction of a second, a fleeting flicker of something-regret, perhaps-but then it was gone. He looked directly at Robert, ignoring David completely. "You know why I'm here. I'll be waiting. You know where to find us." His words hung in the air like a chilling promise before he turned and disappeared into the night, his dark form swallowed by the shadows down the street.

David stared after him for a moment, then slowly closed the door, the click echoing in the sudden, oppressive silence. He turned to look at Robert, who had lost some of his dangerous intensity; instead, a profound weariness etched his face, and his shoulders slumped. He looked grounded now, abruptly returned to the quiet life David offered, but his eyes were wide with worry, fixed on David.

David's own mind raced. What the hell just happened?
