Some people are moving boxes from a moving truck that is parked in front of a different house, in a different neighborhood, in a different state.

The man, now with a cane and a pronounced limp, is directing the movers.

An elderly lady comes over from the house nearby with a plate of cookies.

"Welcome to the neighborhood, dear!" she says, beaming. "I'm Mildred. It's so lovely to have a new face on the street. You just let me know if you need anything at all."

Robert turns to her, a warm smile spreading across his face. "That's so kind of you, Mildred," he says, his voice gentle and sincere as he takes a cookie. "I'm Robert. It's a pleasure to be here."

As Mildred bustles back to her house, Robert's smile slowly fades. He turns around, looking at his new house, a blank canvas.

He takes a bite of the cookie. His eyes distant and thoughtful. He is not planning. He is not remembering. He is simply, quietly, content.

The experiment is over. A new one is about to begin.
